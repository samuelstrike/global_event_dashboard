Introduction

The Earth Observatory Natural Event Tracker (EONET) Dashboard transforms NASA's complex environmental data into accessible visualizations for monitoring and analyzing global natural events. This platform serves as a comprehensive tool for tracking 13 different categories of natural phenomena, from wildfires to volcanic activity, providing real-time insights and historical analysis capabilities.

Problem & Goals

Problem Definition

While EONET provides invaluable data on global natural events, the complexity of this information creates barriers to widespread understanding and utilization.

Goal

To transform complex environmental data into accessible, interactive visual representations that facilitate broader analysis of Earth's dynamic natural processes.

Dataset Overview

Key Characteristics

  • Real-time updates
  • Global geographic scope
  • 13 distinct event categories
  • Precise geospatial data
  • Temporal markers for trend analysis

Key Findings

  • Wildfires are the most frequently recorded events
  • Significant seasonal patterns in event occurrence
  • Geographic clustering of specific event types
  • Temporal trends showing increased frequency

Hypotheses

    • Increased Frequency of Extreme Events: Climate change is leading to increased frequency and intensity of extreme weather events
    • Regional Vulnerability: Certain geographical regions show higher susceptibility to specific natural events
